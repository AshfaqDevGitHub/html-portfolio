The Best Movies According to Muhammad Ashfaq

My top 3 movies of all-time.

Munna Bhai MBBS

The movie Munna Bhai M.B.B.S. revolves around the importance of kindness, compassion, and humanity in the medical profession. The film tells the story of Munna, a gangster who pretends to be a doctor to fulfill his father's dream. Through his journey, the movie highlights the contrast between the rigid, impersonal nature of the medical system and the warmth and empathy that Munna brings to the patients. It underscores the idea that genuine care for others is as important, if not more so, than technical expertise in healing people. The film also explores themes of redemption, the power of love and friendship, and the transformative effect of doing good for others.

3 Idiots

The movie 3 Idiots centers around the themes of friendship, the pressure of the educational system, the pursuit of one's passion, and the importance of thinking creatively and differently. The film follows three friends—Rancho, Farhan, and Raju—during their time at a prestigious engineering college in India.

The movie critiques the rigid and high-pressure environment of academic institutions, which often stifles creativity and individual thought in favor of rote learning and societal expectations. Rancho, the protagonist, encourages his friends to follow their passions rather than succumbing to societal pressures. The film emphasizes that success is not just about achieving high grades or securing prestigious jobs, but about finding fulfillment in what one loves to do.

The movie also explores the dynamics of friendship, the struggles with parental expectations, and the idea that learning should be about understanding concepts and enjoying the process, not just memorizing facts to pass exams. The catchphrase "All is well" symbolizes the belief that facing challenges with a positive mindset can help overcome difficulties.

pk

The movie PK explores themes of religion, faith, and the search for truth. The film follows an alien named PK, who arrives on Earth and embarks on a journey to understand human behavior and the various religious practices he encounters.

Through PK's innocent and often humorous perspective, the movie critiques the blind faith that people sometimes place in religious rituals and the manipulation of faith by religious leaders. It raises questions about the true essence of religion, suggesting that love, compassion, and humanity should be the core values of any faith rather than rigid adherence to rituals and dogmas.

PK also delves into the idea of questioning established norms and not accepting things at face value. PK's journey to find his lost remote (a metaphor for finding one's way) symbolizes the broader search for meaning and truth in life. The movie encourages viewers to think critically about societal norms, religious practices, and the importance of genuine human connections.
